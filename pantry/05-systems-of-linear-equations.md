# Pantry — Chapter 05, Systems of Linear Equations

Source: OpenStax *Elementary Algebra 2e* Ch. 5 (CC-BY 4.0).

## Scenes

- **Two cyclists meeting on a 50-mile road** — used in §1 cold open — captures *intersection-of-constraints* viscerally. Reusable for any "two motions, one meeting point" framing.
- **Small business with candle/mug labor and materials budgets** — used in §5 synthesis — produces an inconsistent system that signals an operational mismatch. Reusable for any "the math identifies a real-world infeasibility" move.

## Analogies

- **System as intersection of two lines** — explains: why systems have one / none / infinite solutions, geometrically — limits: stretches in higher dimensions where intersections of planes can be lines or planes, not just points.
- **Adding equations as applying addition property of equality to both sides at once** — explains: why elimination is legal — limits: glosses the *productivity* (variable disappearing) which is the actual surprise.

## Etymologies

- **System** — Greek *sústēma*, "organized whole" — illuminates: a system is a *coordinated set* of equations, not just a list.
- **Substitution** — Latin *substituere*, "to put in place of" — describes the action.
- **Elimination** — Latin *eliminare*, "to drive out from the threshold" — captures the variable-removal move.
- **Consistent / inconsistent / dependent** — terms imported from logic; *consistent* = has a solution, *inconsistent* = does not, *dependent* = the equations are not independent (one is a scalar multiple of the other).

## Trade-offs named

- **Graphing: visual clarity vs. precision.** Best for seeing the kind of system; worst for exact non-integer answers.
- **Substitution vs. elimination: form-dependent.** Substitution best with isolated variables; elimination best with standard form.
- **More variables, simpler algebra.** Sometimes adding a second unknown linearizes a problem that was quadratic in one unknown. Counterintuitive but durable.

## Scale shifts used

- **Two-by-two systems ↔ n-by-n systems of linear algebra** — purpose: places this chapter as the smallest case of a much bigger machine. Anchored in §1 "Why this chapter matters."

## Worked examples

- **Cyclists on a road** — concept: two motions, intersection point.
- **Substitution: $y = 2x - 3$ and $x + y = 6$ → $(3, 3)$** — concept: substitution method.
- **Elimination: $3x + 2y = 12$ and $5x - 2y = 4$ → $(2, 3)$** — concept: opposites-already cancel.
- **Elimination with scaling: $2x + 3y = 7$ and $5x + 4y = 14$ → $(2, 1)$** — concept: elimination requiring multiplication.
- **Nickels and dimes** — concept: money-problem system setup.
- **Acid mixture (200 mL of 40% from 25%/50%)** — concept: mixture system.
- **Boat with current** — concept: motion-in-medium system.
- **Candle/mug business with inconsistent budgets** — concept: system classification as operational diagnosis.
