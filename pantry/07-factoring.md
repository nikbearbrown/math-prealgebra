## Analogies

- **Factoring as multiplication run backward** — explains: why factoring should feel like reverse FOIL — limits: the analogy collapses for trinomials with $a \ne 1$ where the AC-method's middle-step is not directly visible as "FOIL backward."
- **Zero Product Property as case analysis** — explains: why $AB = 0$ produces two simpler equations — limits: holds only in integral domains; not true in modular arithmetic with composite moduli or in matrix algebra.

## Etymologies

- **Factor** — Latin *facere* (to do, to make) — a factor is something that *makes* the product.
- **Trinomial** — Latin/Greek for *three names* (see chapter 6 pantry).
- **Quadratic** — Latin *quadratus* (squared) — quadratic = involves the square of the variable.
- **GCF** — abbreviation; "greatest common factor."
- **AC method** — informal; named for the product $a \cdot c$ used in the search.

## Trade-offs named

- **Factoring vs. quadratic formula.** Factoring is fast when it works; doesn't work for irreducible trinomials. Chapter 10's formula is universal but slower.
- **AC method vs. trial and error.** AC method always works; sacrifices intuition for reliability.
- **GCF first vs. dive into the trinomial.** GCF first saves time when there's a common factor; if there isn't, it's wasted overhead — but the overhead is small.

## Scale shifts used

- **Real numbers (Zero Product Property holds) ↔ matrix algebra (it doesn't)** — purpose: shows that the chapter's central tool is system-specific. Anchored in §4 reflexive note.

## Worked examples

- **Factoring out GCF: $12x^3y^2 - 18x^2y^4$** — concept: GCF method.
- **Factoring by grouping: $x^3 + 2x^2 + 5x + 10$** — concept: grouping into a common binomial.
- **Trinomial $x^2 + 7x + 12 = (x+3)(x+4)$** — concept: two-numbers method for $a = 1$.
- **Trinomial $x^2 - x - 12 = (x-4)(x+3)$** — concept: opposite-sign case.
- **AC method: $2x^2 + 7x + 3 = (x+3)(2x+1)$** — concept: AC method for $a \ne 1$.
- **Difference of squares: $9x^2 - 25 = (3x+5)(3x-5)$** — pattern recognition.
- **Perfect square: $x^2 + 10x + 25 = (x+5)^2$** — pattern recognition.
- **Complete factoring: $2x^3 - 8x = 2x(x+2)(x-2)$** — concept: GCF first, then continue.
- **Solving by factoring: $x^2 + 5x + 6 = 0$** — concept: Zero Product Property in action.
- **Rectangle area problem (6 by 8 inches)** — concept: word problem with factoring.
- **Business break-even ($-2x^2 + 80x - 600 = 0$)** — concept: factoring + GCF + interpretation.
