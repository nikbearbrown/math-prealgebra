# Figure briefs — Chapter 08, Rational Expressions and Equations

No `[FIGURE: ...]` placeholders inserted. The chapter is heavily symbolic; potential figure candidates listed below.

If a designer reinstates figures, candidates would be:

- **Domain visualization on a number line.** A horizontal number line for $\frac{x^2 - 4}{x^2 + 5x + 6}$ with open circles at $x = -3$ and $x = -2$, indicating the forbidden values. Pedagogical purpose: anchor the abstract notion of "domain" to a visual representation.
- **The rate-addition diagram.** Two horizontal arrows representing the rates $1/6$ and $1/4$ of a job per hour, summing to $5/12$ per hour. Pedagogical purpose: visualize rate addition for the paving-machine setup.
- **Cancel-only-factors infographic.** A side-by-side comparison: left panel shows $\frac{6 \cdot x}{6}$ with the $6$'s canceled to leave $x$ ✓; right panel shows $\frac{x + 6}{6}$ with a struck-through cancellation attempt ✗ and the note "the 6 is added, not multiplied; cannot cancel." Pedagogical purpose: reinforce the most expensive error in the chapter.
- **The extraneous-solution flowchart.** A small decision tree: solve cleared equation → list candidates → for each candidate, ask "does it make any original denominator zero?" → reject if yes, accept (after verification) if no. Pedagogical purpose: encode the check procedure visually.

The OpenStax source (m82532, m82534, m82538) contains step-by-step worked-example illustrations rather than conceptual figures. Those could be reinstated in published form for visual learners.
