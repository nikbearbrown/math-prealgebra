## Figure 1: A parabola with all features labeled

**Placement:** §4 (Concept 3 — Graphing parabolas), after the worked example for $y = x^2 - 4x + 3$.

**Description:** A coordinate plane spanning x from approximately $-2$ to $+6$, y from $-2$ to $+5$. The parabola $y = x^2 - 4x + 3$ is plotted as a smooth U-shape opening upward.

Mark and label:
- **Vertex** at $(2, -1)$, with a solid dot and label "vertex (2, −1)" positioned to the lower right.
- **Axis of symmetry** as a dashed vertical line at $x = 2$, extending from below the parabola to above; label "axis of symmetry $x = 2$" along the line near the top.
- **X-intercepts** at $(1, 0)$ and $(3, 0)$, with solid dots and labels above each, "$(1, 0)$" and "$(3, 0)$".
- **Y-intercept** at $(0, 3)$, with a solid dot and label "$(0, 3)$" to the left.
- **Symmetric point** at $(4, 3)$, with a hollow dot (to indicate it's a derived point from symmetry) and label "$(4, 3)$" to the right; a small horizontal arrow connecting it to the y-intercept showing the equal-height symmetry.

**Pedagogical purpose:** Show, in a single image, all five algebraic features of a parabola located in the coordinate plane: vertex, axis of symmetry, x-intercepts, y-intercept, and one symmetric point. Anchor the formulas $x = -b/(2a)$, "$y$-intercept $= c$," and "x-intercepts are roots of the quadratic" to specific marked points.

**Style notes:** Smooth curve drawn in solid black ink. Coordinate axes with arrows and integer tick marks. Vertex dot slightly larger than the others. Dashed axis of symmetry to distinguish it from the solid axes. Clear labels positioned to avoid overlap. Equal scale on both axes.

**Source link:** Conceptually parallels OpenStax m82559 figures, which depict similar features but spread across multiple smaller figures. The rewrite consolidates into one canonical reference.
