# Figure briefs — Chapter 03, Math Models

No `[FIGURE: ...]` placeholders were inserted in the chapter; mixture-table and motion-table layouts are rendered as Markdown tables in the body text rather than as figures.

If a future revision wants illustrations, candidates would be:

- **A schematic of the saline mixture problem** — two beakers (one labeled "50% saline, x mL", one "water, 200−x mL") combining into a third beaker labeled "200 mL, 30% saline." Pedagogical purpose: make the conservation principle visible (active ingredient in equals active ingredient out).
- **Two cars on a horizontal line, arrows pointing in opposite directions** — labeled with rates and a bracket showing "360 miles apart" between them. Pedagogical purpose: anchor the additive-distance interpretation of opposite-direction motion problems.
- **A right triangle with legs 9 and 12 labeled, hypotenuse marked unknown** — for the Pythagorean exercise. Pedagogical purpose: visualize the configuration before the algebra.

The OpenStax source contains these and similar figures. They were not pulled into the rewrite to keep the focus on the modeling structure rather than on geometry visualization, but a designer producing the published version may want to reinstate them.
