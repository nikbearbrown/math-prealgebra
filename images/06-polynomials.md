# Figure briefs — Chapter 06, Polynomials

No `[FIGURE: ...]` placeholders were inserted. The chapter's content is heavily symbolic; tables in the body text (classification table, exponent-rules table) carry most of the visual load.

If a designer reinstates figures, candidates would be:

- **Pizza area comparison.** Two pizzas drawn to scale (10-inch and 14-inch), with their areas computed and displayed alongside. Pedagogical purpose: anchor the square-scaling argument that opens §1. The 14-inch pizza should look visibly almost-twice-as-much-pizza as the 10-inch.
- **Polynomial-degree visual taxonomy.** Five labeled examples — degree 0 (constant), degree 1 (linear), degree 2 (quadratic), degree 3 (cubic), degree 4 (quartic) — each with one example polynomial. Pedagogical purpose: associate degree with name.
- **FOIL pattern.** A diagram showing $(a + b)(c + d)$ with arcs connecting the four pairs of terms (F: a-c, O: a-d, I: b-c, L: b-d). Pedagogical purpose: visualize the four-product expansion.
- **Polynomial long division layout.** A worked example of $x^2 + 5x + 6$ divided by $x + 2$, formatted exactly as a student would write it. Pedagogical purpose: model the algorithm step by step.
- **Scientific notation number line.** A horizontal scale showing $10^{-31}$ (electron mass) on the far left, $10^0 = 1$ in the center, and $10^{24}$ (Earth mass) on the far right, with several labeled examples in between. Pedagogical purpose: anchor scientific notation visually as a way of compressing scale.

The OpenStax source contains figures for area models of polynomial multiplication (m82507, m82508), which are pedagogically useful for visual learners and could be adapted for the binomial-square pattern in particular.
