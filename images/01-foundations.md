## Figure 1: Absolute value as distance

**Placement:** §2 (Concept 1 — The number line), after the paragraph defining absolute value as distance from zero.

**Description:** A horizontal number line spanning at least −10 to +10, with integer ticks labeled. Above the line, two arc segments are drawn:
- One arc spanning from 0 leftward to −7, labeled with the length "7".
- One arc spanning from 0 rightward to +7, labeled with the length "7".
Below the line, two annotations: "$|-7| = 7$" placed beneath the −7 tick, and "$|7| = 7$" placed beneath the +7 tick.

**Pedagogical purpose:** Show, visually, that the absolute value symbol records *distance* — and that distance is the same in both directions. The arcs intentionally span the same physical length on the page, so the eye sees that −7 and +7 are equidistant from zero and that the absolute value of either is the same nonnegative number.

**Style notes:** Diagrammatic, clean line art. Clear tick marks at every integer. The two arcs should be distinguishable but visually parallel — same line weight, same thickness, just located on different sides. Labels in mathematical notation (LaTeX-style). Avoid color coding by sign; the figure is about distance being symmetric, not about negative being "different."

**Dimensions:** Roughly 4:1 aspect ratio (wide rectangle).

**Source link:** Conceptually replaces OpenStax CNX_ElemAlg_Figure_01_03_001_new and 01_03_002_new from m82454 (which depicted ordering and sign separately). The rewrite consolidates both ideas into one symmetric figure.

## Figure 2: Properties of real numbers vs. properties of 2×2 matrix multiplication

**Placement:** §4 (Concept 3 — Properties of real numbers), after the reflexive note that "these 'properties' are not laws of nature."

**Description:** A two-column comparison table.

Left column header: **Real numbers under + and ×**
- Commutative: ✓
- Associative: ✓
- Identity: ✓ (0 for +, 1 for ×)
- Inverse: ✓ (0 has no multiplicative inverse — flagged)
- Distributive: ✓

Right column header: **2×2 matrix multiplication**
- Commutative: ✗ ($AB \ne BA$ in general)
- Associative: ✓
- Identity: ✓ (the identity matrix $I$)
- Inverse: ✓ (only when the matrix is invertible)
- Distributive: ✓

**Pedagogical purpose:** Make visible that the five properties are properties of a *system*, not laws of nature. Different mathematical systems satisfy different subsets. This sets up the reflexive note in the chapter and primes students for later encounters with non-commutative algebras.

**Style notes:** Clean two-column table with bold checks (✓) in green and crosses (✗) in muted red, or both in the same dark color if color is constrained. Subtle horizontal rules between properties. The footnote callouts ("0 has no mult. inverse" and "$AB \ne BA$ in general") sit immediately under the relevant row, not at the bottom of the figure. No header background fill — keep visual weight on the content.

**Dimensions:** Square or 4:3 aspect ratio. Designed to fit alongside body text or as a single-column figure.

**Source link:** This figure has no direct counterpart in the OpenStax source. It is original to the rewrite and earns its place by making the reflexive point — that the properties are properties of *this* system, not features of mathematics in general.
