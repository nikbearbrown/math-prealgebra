# Figure briefs — Chapter 07, Factoring

No `[FIGURE: ...]` placeholders inserted. The chapter is heavily symbolic; visualization candidates noted below.

If a designer reinstates figures, candidates would be:

- **Decision tree for the general factoring strategy.** A flowchart that branches on (a) is there a GCF? (b) how many terms? (c) which pattern? Pedagogical purpose: encode the §3 strategy visually so students can follow the path on a confusing problem.
- **The baseball trajectory.** A parabola $h(t) = -16t^2 + 64t$ plotted with the two zeros ($t = 0$ and $t = 4$) marked, and the maximum ($t = 2$, $h = 64$) labeled. Pedagogical purpose: show that factored form $-16t(t - 4)$ identifies the zeros directly, and that the vertex sits midway between them.
- **Area model for $x^2 + 5x + 6 = (x+2)(x+3)$.** A rectangle of side $(x+2)$ by $(x+3)$ partitioned into four sub-rectangles of areas $x^2, 3x, 2x, 6$. Pedagogical purpose: anchor the algebraic factoring in a visible geometric decomposition. (OpenStax m82566 has similar area-model figures.)
- **Difference of squares as a geometric subtraction.** Two squares: a large square of side $a$ and a smaller square of side $b$ removed from a corner. The L-shaped remaining region has area $a^2 - b^2$, which can be rearranged into a rectangle of dimensions $(a+b) \times (a-b)$. Pedagogical purpose: visualize the algebraic identity geometrically. This is a classical figure dating back to ancient Greek geometry.
- **Profit parabola for the §5 synthesis.** $P(x) = -2x^2 + 80x - 600$ plotted with the two break-even points $x = 10$ and $x = 30$ marked, the profit region between them shaded, and the maximum profit at $x = 20$, $P = 200$ labeled. Pedagogical purpose: connect the algebra to the business-decision interpretation.

The OpenStax source contains some area-model figures for trinomial factoring (m82566) which are pedagogically valuable. They were not pulled into the rewrite to keep the focus on the algebraic strategy, but a designer producing the published version may want to reinstate them for visual learners.
