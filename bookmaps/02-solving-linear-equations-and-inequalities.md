## Deferred material

- **Distance, rate, and time formula** (from m82469) — peripheral to the spine of solving equations. Deferred to chapter 3 (math models), where it joins percent, mixture, and uniform-motion templates.
- **Solving a formula for a specific variable (literal equations)** (also m82469) — useful but not central to chapter 2's solving narrative. Deferred to chapter 3 or to a later chapter on functions, where it can be motivated as the work of "express $y$ in terms of $x$."
- **Translation tables and word-problem applications throughout m82463–m82465 and m82470** — extensive in source but compressed in rewrite to a single salesperson example in §5 plus exercises 9–11. Reason: chapter 3 is the *math models* chapter and is the natural home for word-problem variety.
- **Multiple worked translation examples** (e.g., "the difference of a number and 12 is 8") — present in source as exercise drills. Compressed in rewrite to keep §3's strategy section focused on procedure rather than translation. Translation-as-skill is treated more deeply in chapter 3.

## Source-level notes

- **No contradictions** between source files were observed. The sequencing in source — first add/sub, then mult/div, then both sides, then general strategy, then fractions/decimals, then inequalities — is pedagogically sound, but the rewrite reorders into three concepts for narrative coherence: properties → strategy + classification → inequalities. The same content survives.
- **Flip rule justification.** OpenStax m82470 states the flip rule and demonstrates by example, without giving the reflection-across-zero geometric argument used in the rewrite §4. The geometric argument is original to this rewrite and is consistent with standard mathematical reasoning about ordered fields.
- **Mixture problem.** Exercise 9 references a 0.06/0.04 acid-mixture problem from m82468's worked-example bank. The rewrite uses a similar template (0.05/0.20/0.10) for §3's clearing-decimals worked example.
- **Salesperson synthesis problem (§5).** Original construction; standard income-with-commission template, not lifted verbatim from source.
