## Source-level notes

- **No contradictions** between source files.
- **Principal-root convention.** Source uses the convention without naming it as a convention. The rewrite makes the convention explicit and notes its role in simplifying notation.
- **Pendulum synthesis (§5).** Original construction. Formula $T = 2\pi\sqrt{L/32}$ matches the standard physics result $T = 2\pi\sqrt{L/g}$ with $g$ in feet-per-second-squared (32 ft/s²). The numerical answer ($L = 8/\pi^2 \approx 0.811$ ft ≈ 9.7 inches for $T = 1$ second) matches the textbook standard "1-Hz pendulum is roughly 10 inches long."
- **Cliff cold open.** Original framing in the rewrite; the height-time relationship is universal physics.
- **Rationalize-the-denominator reflexive note.** The historical-artifact framing is original to the rewrite; OpenStax presents rationalization as a fixed convention without commentary.
